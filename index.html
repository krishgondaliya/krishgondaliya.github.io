This site is a collection of projects, notes, experiments, and ideas I've accumulated over time while learning and building things that genuinely interest me. Lately I've been spending a lot of time exploring software design, backend infrastructure, distributed systems, performance optimization, and effectively using modern C++.

I enjoy building things from scratch, improving how they work, and understanding why certain designs hold up better than others. A lot of my projects start from curiosity: testing an idea, digging into a technical detail, or trying to understand a system deeply enough to rebuild part of it myself.

Some things here are finished projects, while others are ongoing explorations, research notes, or half-formed ideas that I thought were worth documenting anyway.

I've always liked how the internet makes curiosity feel cumulative. You follow one interesting idea long enough and eventually it changes the kinds of problems you want to spend your time thinking about.
